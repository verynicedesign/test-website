Context

A process that cost them sales every day

Norman's Nursery is a 1,000-person wholesale nursery based in Southern California with multiple yard locations. Their old process for capturing and sharing plant inventory photos was entirely manual — images lived on personal phones, communication happened via text or email, and customers often waited days for photos that should take minutes.

The lag wasn't just inconvenient: it was directly measurable in lost orders. By the time photos reached the customer, the opportunity was often gone — lost to vendors who could respond faster.

Research

Listening before designing anything

Before wireframing a single screen, I interviewed four team members across departments — the COO, Inventory Manager, Tagging Coordinator, and a Sales Rep. Their pain points shaped every product decision.

The COO: "The biggest frustration is the time it takes to get photos back. We don't have the staff to do all of the photos." She needed a scalable system, not more headcount.

The Sales Rep: "If I could get pictures to my clients faster, I'd close 20–25% more orders." Speed wasn't a nice-to-have — it was a competitive advantage.

Plants are living inventory. They change daily — growing, dying, selling. Keeping photos current across a multi-yard operation is impossible without real-time tooling. Every design decision had to account for the fact that the data would be constantly changing.

Role Architecture

Four distinct roles, one connected system

Each user type has a specific job in the inventory workflow — and the app needed to serve all of them without adding friction. The system architecture maps each role to a platform optimized for where and how they work.

Validation

Testing with real users, not assumptions

Ran usability sessions with six participants covering registration, plant search, yard selection, photo upload, and data submission. Feedback shaped significant improvements.

What validated: The capture-to-cloud flow worked intuitively for yard workers. Sales team search-and-download was fast. The shared database eliminated the back-and-forth communication entirely.

What we refined: Photo upload button placement caused confusion about task sequence — redesigned before next round. Navigation labels needed simplification. The camera tool integration needed streamlining for multi-photo uploads. Added bilingual support for Spanish-speaking yard workers. + Bilingual support added · EN / ES

Results

Results that moved the business

62% faster photo turnaround — from hours to minutes. 48% reduction in outdated photos reaching customers. 3× more photos shared across all yard locations. For the first time, all departments share a single source of truth.

The iOS app launched first — putting the most critical workflow (photo capture) in the hands of yard workers immediately. The web application followed, completing the full cross-department experience. Leadership gained real visibility into inventory volume and team throughput, and the platform now supports the full inventory lifecycle from capture to archive across a 1,000-person operation.
